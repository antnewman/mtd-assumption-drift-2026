## Narrative

HMRC's total transitional cost estimate for the above-£30k mandated population is £561m in 2021 prices, stated on the 22 February 2024 TIIN. The per-TIIN breakdown is £223m for the above-£50k threshold cohort and £338m for the £30k-£50k cohort.

Repriced to {comparison_date} using the ONS services CPI index (D7F5), the current-price equivalent is approximately £{comparison_value:.2f}. This is a shift of {drift_absolute:+.2f} against the £561m baseline, or {drift_percent:+.2f}%.

The aggregate reprice uses the same index and formula as the per-business reprice in §6.1, so the percentage shift is the same. The aggregate is reported separately because it is the figure that appears in the overall business case and HM Treasury Green Book appraisal, and because a reader comparing the investigation against HMRC's public documents will look for the £561m figure specifically.

## Methodological caveats

**§7.1 services CPI vs all-items CPI.** As with §6.1, the reprice uses services CPI rather than all-items CPI. Services inflation ran above headline CPI throughout the period of interest; the choice materially affects the drift value.
